Habit Hijacker

I got the idea for this project after reading "The Power of Habit" by Charles Duhigg. This is a book that explains the science behind habits, and how these inform not only how individuals behave but can also explain organisational success and social movements. Insipired by the principles behind habit formation explained in the book, I created this app to assist me in changing my own habits. It's a tool that helps me identify my habit cues, understand my routines, and capture any progress I am making.

Technologies used: Dart with Flutter

This Website

A website? What's special about that? Well, unlike most sites, it's built entirely as an interactive SVG. That means every section is part of a single dynamic image. The idea came when I first learned about Mike Bostock's D3.js library, back when I was starting my Ph.D. and was just learning JavaScript. At the time, D3 was making the New York Times' data visualizations stand out (today, it's used everywhere), and I thought: "that would make for a beautiful website". The code is messy and undocumented, as I built this website as a way to learn JavaScript and D3.js, so level your expectations accordingly.

Technologies used: D3.js, JavaScript, HTML/CSS
